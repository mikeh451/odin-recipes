Crispy Fried Chicken

Cooking chicken is always delicate, but luckily I find making fried chicken is more forgiving.

It all starts with the right breading and a hot pot of oil. I recoomened peanut oil as it high as much higher smoke point.

Ingredients

  • 2lbs chicken. I like thighs but all cuts work
  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 cup corn starch
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 Tbsp salt
  • 2 Tbsp pepper
  • 1 Tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 Tbsp paprika
  • 1-3 quarts oil for frying. Depends on size of pot
  • Sauce which I like equal parts Frank's Redhot and Sweet Baby Ray's BBQ.
  • 3 Tbsp Butter

Steps

  1. Heat oil in large pot on stove on medium high heat. 375 degrees is goal
  2. Cut chicken into manageble pieces. 2 inch squares is ideal
  3. Combind flour, corn starch, and spices in large bowl
  4. Prepare egg wash by whisking eggs and milk in separate, large bowl
  5. Cover chicken in flour mix completely
  6. Bathe flour covered chicken in egg wash
  7. Repeat step 5
  8. Slowly lower chicken in hot oil. Lightly stir to make sure pieces do not stick
  9. Cook 14-18 minutes, until chicken internal temp reaches 160 degrees
  10. Prepare sauce in large bowl starting with softened butter and both sauces
  11. Remove chicken from oil and place onto plate with paper towel for 2 minutes
  12. Toss hot chicken in suace until fully coated
